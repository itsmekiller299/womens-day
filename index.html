Women who changed the world

🔬

Marie Curie

Physicist & Chemist

First woman to win a Nobel Prize and the only person to win in two sciences. Her work on radioactivity saved countless lives.

✊

Rosa Parks

Civil Rights Activist

Her refusal to give up her bus seat in 1955 sparked the Montgomery Bus Boycott and became a symbol of the fight for equality.

📜

Ruth Bader Ginsburg

Supreme Court Justice

Fought for gender equality through the law. Her dissents and opinions shaped rights for women in the United States.

🩺

Katherine Johnson

NASA Mathematician

Calculated trajectories for Apollo 11. Her brilliance helped put humans on the moon and broke barriers for Black women in STEM.

Did you know?

Origin

International Women's Day has been observed since the early 1900s. The first National Woman's Day was in the United States in 1909.

Purple, green & white

These colors symbolize justice (purple), hope (green), and purity (white). They were used by the Women's Social and Political Union in the UK in 1908.

Global holiday

In many countries—including Afghanistan, Russia, and Vietnam—March 8 is an official public holiday.

UN recognition

The United Nations officially recognized International Women's Day in 1975 and has given it a theme each year since 1996.

#EachForEqual

Recent themes focus on collective action: "Each for Equal" (2020) and "Break the Bias" (2022) remind us that equality is everyone's responsibility.
